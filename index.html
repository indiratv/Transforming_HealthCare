    Epidemic Outbreak Prediction:

    1. Promed-mail is an Internet-based reporting system dedicated to rapid global dissemination of information on outbreaks of infectious diseases and acute exposures to toxins that affect human health, including those in animals and in plants grown for food or animal feed.

    2. By providing early warning of outbreaks of emerging and re-emerging diseases, public health precautions at all levels can be taken in a timely manner to prevent epidemic transmission and to save lives.

    3. A central purpose of ProMED is to promote communication amongst the international infectious disease community, including scientists, physicians, epidemiologists, public health professionals, and others interested in infectious diseases on a global scale.

    Diagnosis and Medical Imaging:
    Recently there has been a new surge in developing machine learning applications in the analysis of medical imaging.
    One common examples is the use of Convolutional Neural Networks (CNN).
    CNNs have been trained to be able to analyze each individual voxel in a patient’s 3-Dimensional MRI scan to determine which areas of the image contain pathological findings (tumor, sclerosis, ischemia, etc.) and which do not.
    Successful implementation of these type of algorithms will facilitate both faster and finer detection of dangerous diseases.




    Preventive Care:

    • Performed a deep classification analysis on cardiovascular disease and its associated risk factors.
    • The analysis was performed based on age, gender, race, and topics related to types of cardiovascular disease and stroke.
    • Cluster Analysis on various risk factors for e.g. Cholesterol abnormalities, physical inactivity, obesity, hypertension, smoking, diabetes and others over the period of 5 years.
    • Heatmap on risk score based on several breakout categories (age, race, gender, etc…)

    Machine Learning Model (Randomforest Algorithm based on Decision Tree)
    • Used a Randomforest Algorithm to predict stroke based on randomly created decision tree based on several classifiers (e.g. age, gender, hypertension, hear disease. marital status, work type, residence, average glucose level)
    • Used a dataset from CDC of approx. 45K rows over 5 years with multiple classifiers (features)
    • Predicted randomforest classifier score and generated a decision tree
    • Sorted each feature by their respective score. Code




    Disease Identification/Diagnosis:

    • Created and compared six models based on our data. Models used were:
    • Logistic Regression, Decision Tree Classifier, K Neighbors, SVC, Linear Discriminant Analysis and Gaussian NB
    • Stroke data with 43400 records, heart disease with 303 records, hepatitis with 305 records and Parkinsons disease with 195 records were used.
    • Accuracy score for training and testing data was generated and best model for each disease was identified to predict the outcome. Learned what factors drive each prediction.
    • Stroke data was truncated to 3260 and 434 records to study the effect of record size. Comparison plots were generated. Large data size(>10k) provided better results.
    • Stroke data with 4300 records was also used to study the effect of 10/90, 20/80, 30/70 and 40/60 split of dataset into test/train data. Comparison plots generated.  Code




    Insurance: 
    Customers provide extensive information to identify risk classification and eligibility, including scheduling medical exams, a process that takes an average of 30 days.
    The result? People are turned off. That’s why only 40% of U.S. households own individual life insurance.
    Prudential wants to make it quicker and less labor intensive for new and existing customers to get a quote while maintaining privacy boundaries.
    By developing a predictive model that accurately classifies risk using a more automated approach, we can greatly impact public perception of the industry.  Code
